Lesson 5: Affixes

The -s suffix is always written as the S stroke. Even if it makes the Z sound[15]. The -ses suffix is written as S with an intersection[16].

The -ed suffix is written as a disjoined D stroke. If the root word ends in D (hence -ded) then an intersection is written through the last D to represent the -ed suffix[17].

The -y suffix (making the long E sound) is written as a final vowel dot[18].

The ing(s), tion(s), and -ly suffixes are all written as a terminal loop. They are distinguished by the context they are written in[19].

The following letters can represent affixes if written disjoined to the rest of the word[20].

  • B: -ble
  • F: -ful, for-
  • M: -ment(s)
  • N: -ness
  • S: -self-
  • W: -ward
  • Sh: -ship-
  • Loop O: -out-
